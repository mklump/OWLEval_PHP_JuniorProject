Final Presentation

  • Project Introduction
    • What is the system you created intended to do? Provide a portable, web-based, secure evaluation system by the end of Spring term 2004.
    • What does it actually do?
    • Show your initial timeline and project plan.
  • Business Case
    • Why did you decide to build this system?
    • Who is your sponsor and why do they want the system?
    • How does your system benefit the end users?
    • Who are your target users?
  • Requirements
    • How did you gather requirements?
    • What are the requirements?
    • How are your requirements organized (hierarchy, traceability, etc.)?
    • Show your requirements model and explain how it is used.
      • Clearly define functional requirements
      • Clearly define non-functional requirements
      • Explain how functional and non-functional requirements relate to your finished system.
  • Use Case Model to reflect your final implementation
    • How did your derive your use cases?
    • Show your use case model and explain how your use cases map to your requirements.
    • Explain the structure of your use case model and how it supports your requirements model.
    • Show and explain at least two of your key scenarios and indicate how these scenarios are important for the overall architecture of the system.
  • System Architecture
    • Present your system physical architecture.
    • Present your system logical architecture.
    • Present your application architecture (e.g. threading models, client-server, web based, COM+, etc.).
    • Show how your architecture supports your non-functional requirements and your use case model.
  • Class Model
    • For each logical package
      • Present all class models
      • Explain key classes and how they were implemented
    • Describe how your class model supports your use case model
    • Describe how you derived code from the class model
  • System Map
    • Present your system map
    • Perform an impact analysis on one of the areas in the system that is most likely to change using the system map.
  • Code deliverable
    • Show some of your favorite code snippets.
    • Explain the challenges in doing the implementation
      • What were your major roadblocks?
      • How did you overcome those roadblocks?
      • What are the unique areas in your end implementation?
      • What parts did you enjoy most?
      • What parts were the worse?
    • How many lines of code make up your final project?
  • Demo
    • How is your system installed?
    • Show any installation scripts you had to write.
    • Show how your system works in detail.
      • As you demonstrate your system:
        • Explain exactly what you are doing
        • Explain what your audience is seeing on the screen
        • Explain any significant events that are happening internal to your system
  • Post Mortem Session
    • What went right with your project?
    • What were some of the pitfalls or lessons that you learned in doing this project
    • If you could do this over again, what would you do differently?
    • Describe the phases that your team went through and how that affected your project schedule and productivity.
  • Question/Answer Session
